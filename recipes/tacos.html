Tacos

Some Taco basics:

  • Tacos are quite straight forward. Just put some meat and toppings on a tortilla. That's it. The beauty and the possibilities that come out of simplicity.
  • No need to be a chef to know that good ingredients make the difference. Forget about store-bought tortillas. Just, don't.
    And if you have ever tried making a tortilla, you know it is simple to learn, difficult to master.
  • Best case scenario: your mexican or central american mother or grandmother made some tortillas to you. If you don't have such grandma, then master the art of making tortillas.
    Pro tip: if it starts inflating and becomes a big bubble when you flip it in the pan, you're doing it right. Good luck.

Got good tortillas, what's next?

Here are some basics you're going to need or want:

  1. You know the first step. Tortillas. The fresher the better.
  2. Add meat to a tortilla. You can make veggie tacos if you wish, but they generally have meat.
    Pulled pork, carne asada, beef, chorizo, chicken, even fish... Your choice
  3. Chop some onions and coriander and sprinkle them over the meat in the tortilla. Some people like adding small pineapple chunks too.
  4. Pico de gallo. Which is fine chopped tomato, onion, coriander, salt and pepper, all mixed.
  5. Last but not least: sauce. You know mexicans need that spicy kick, but you can also just add green or red sauce.

Wrap it up a little bit and have a it!
